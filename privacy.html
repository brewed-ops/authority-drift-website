2. Information We Collect

2.1 Information You Provide Directly

  • Identity and contact data - name, email address, mailing address, phone number, and billing information when you purchase a product, subscribe to our email list, apply to a cohort, register for an event, or contact us
  • Payment data - processed by our payment provider (Stripe). We do not store full credit card numbers on our servers. We receive transaction metadata (last four digits, card brand, transaction ID, purchase amount)
  • Account data - login credentials for our customer portal, community, or course platform
  • Survey and application responses - information you provide on intake forms, cohort applications, feedback surveys, and contact forms
  • Communications - emails, voicemails, SMS messages, and direct messages you send us

2.2 Information Collected Automatically

  • Device and browser data - IP address, browser type, operating system, device identifiers, screen resolution, language preferences
  • Usage data - pages viewed, time spent, referring URLs, click paths, search terms used on our Sites
  • Location data - approximate geographic location derived from IP address (country, region, city level)
  • Cookie and tracking data - see Section 5 for details

2.3 Information from Third Parties

  • Social media platforms - when you interact with our content on LinkedIn, Facebook, Instagram, or YouTube, we may receive engagement data from those platforms
  • Service providers - our CRM, email platform, and analytics providers furnish us with aggregate reporting data
  • Referrals and testimonials - if you are introduced to us by an existing customer, we may receive your contact information

3. How We Use Your Information

  • Fulfill purchases and deliver digital and physical products
  • Provide customer support and respond to inquiries
  • Send transactional communications (order confirmations, receipts, shipping notifications, account updates)
  • Send marketing communications you have opted into (newsletters, product launches, event invitations)
  • Operate and improve our Sites, products, and services
  • Personalize your experience and recommend relevant content
  • Analyze usage patterns and measure marketing effectiveness
  • Prevent fraud, enforce our terms, and protect our legal rights
  • Comply with legal obligations (tax, accounting, subpoenas)

Legal basis (for EU visitors): Where applicable, we process personal data based on: (a) your consent, (b) our contractual obligations to you, (c) compliance with legal obligations, and (d) our legitimate business interests.

5. Cookies and Tracking Technologies

Our Sites use cookies, pixels, tags, and similar technologies to keep you logged in, analyze site traffic (Google Analytics, GTM), serve relevant advertising (Meta Pixel, LinkedIn Insight Tag, Google Ads), and track marketing campaign effectiveness.

Types of cookies:

  • Strictly necessary - required for basic site operation (checkout, login)
  • Analytics - measure how visitors use our Sites
  • Advertising - serve relevant ads and measure ad performance
  • Functional - remember preferences and personalize content

How to control cookies: Manage cookies through your browser settings. Disabling certain cookies may affect site functionality. Our Sites do not currently respond to "Do Not Track" browser signals, but we respect Global Privacy Control (GPC) signals where required by state law.

6. Your Privacy Rights

Subject to applicable law, you have the right to:

  • Access - request a copy of the personal information we hold about you
  • Correct - request correction of inaccurate or incomplete information
  • Delete - request deletion of your personal information
  • Port - request your information in a portable, machine-readable format
  • Opt out of marketing - unsubscribe from marketing emails at any time
  • Withdraw consent - where processing is based on consent

To exercise any of these rights, email team@authoritydriftbook.com with the subject line "Privacy Request." We will respond within 45 days. We will not discriminate against you for exercising your privacy rights.

7. California Privacy Rights (CCPA / CPRA)

California residents have additional rights under the CCPA / CPRA: the right to know what categories of personal information we collect, the right to delete, the right to correct, the right to opt out of sale or sharing for cross-context behavioral advertising, the right to limit use of sensitive personal information, and the right to non-discrimination.

To opt out of sharing for cross-context behavioral advertising, email team@authoritydriftbook.com with "Do Not Sell or Share My Info" in the subject line. We do not sell personal information for monetary consideration. We "share" identifiers and internet activity with advertising platforms (Meta, Google, LinkedIn) for cross-context behavioral advertising.

8. Other U.S. State Privacy Rights

Residents of Virginia (VCDPA), Colorado (CPA), Connecticut (CTDPA), Utah (UCPA), Texas (TDPSA), Montana, Oregon, and other states with comprehensive privacy laws have similar rights to access, correct, delete, and port personal data, and to opt out of targeted advertising and sale of personal data. To exercise these rights, email team@authoritydriftbook.com.

9. International Visitors and GDPR

Our Sites are operated from the United States. If you visit from outside the U.S., your information will be transferred to, processed, and stored in the United States. EEA, UK, and Swiss residents have rights under GDPR including the right to lodge a complaint with a local data protection authority. Cross-border transfers rely on appropriate safeguards (such as Standard Contractual Clauses).

10. Children's Privacy

Our Sites and services are intended for adults (age 18 and older). We do not knowingly collect personal information from children under 13. If you believe we have, contact us at team@authoritydriftbook.com.

11. Data Retention

We retain personal information for as long as necessary to provide our services, maintain active customer relationships, comply with tax / accounting / legal obligations (typically 7 years for financial records), and enforce our agreements. Then we securely delete or anonymize it.

12. Data Security

We maintain reasonable administrative, technical, and physical safeguards: encrypted data transmission (SSL/TLS), access controls, encrypted payment processing through Stripe (PCI-DSS compliant), secure authentication, and regular review of our security practices. No method of transmission or electronic storage is 100% secure.
